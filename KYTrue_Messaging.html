Five core messages

The five things
that must land

Every conversation, deck, and email should reinforce at least one of these. They are the building blocks, not talking points. When all five land, the investment decision becomes obvious.

01
The problem is real, big, and getting worse
Identity fraud exceeds $380B annually. The KYC market is $32B and underserved. Every solution built in the last 15 years authenticates a credential, not the living person. AI deepfakes now bypass the best liveness checks. The market has no answer. KYTrue is the answer.
“$380 billion is larger than the GDP of Denmark. And every cent of it flows through a flaw that existing providers cannot fix: because their model is wrong at its foundation.”
02
The technology is clinically proven, not a prototype
The Revealense Illuminator® is operational in medical and defence environments at absolute precision. 28 peer-reviewed clinical studies. The engine analyses haemodynamic blood-flow patterns that cannot be synthesised, replayed, or injected by a deepfake. This is not a demo. It is a validated platform looking for commercial distribution.
“We are not building the science. The science is built. We are commercialising it.”
03
The regulatory moment is now; structural, not cyclical
MiCA (December 2024), eIDAS 2.0 (2026), AMLD6, FATF Travel Rule, US Digital Asset Act, and CBDC pilots create a mandatory compliance upgrade cycle affecting every regulated financial institution globally. Regulated buyers must upgrade. KYTrue is purpose-built for the LoA-High standard. This is a compliance tailwind, not an optional market.
04
The business model mirrors Visa, a a toll on identity
KYTrue does not compete with payment networks. It sits beneath all of them as the identity primitive, the same role TLS occupies for transport. Identity Rail™ inserts the kyt_id into every payment message. Every transaction that carries a kyt_id is a toll that belongs to KYTrue. This is infrastructure economics, not SaaS.
05
The moat compounds: every verification widens the lead
Three PCT patents. A haemodynamic training dataset that is years away from being replicated. A regulatory standard that, once certified, means competitors must comply with KYTrue’s architecture. And a network effect: every new institution that accepts the kyt_id Passport makes the credential more valuable to every existing holder. The lead widens with every session.

Message discipline

What to say.
What never to say.

Investor conversations are won and lost on phrasing. These are the most common patterns that build or destroy credibility in a single sentence.

Always say
“We commercialise a defence-grade engine. We do not invent the science; it is 28 studies deep.”
“The regulatory tailwind is mandatory, not optional. MiCA is already in force.”
“The moat compounds: every verification widens the data lead over any potential replicant.”
“We are raising $22M to make kyt_id the standard, not to finish the technology.”
“The $137M Year 5 ARR is the conservative case, built from verified segment ACVs.”
“The sincerity engine has no direct commercial equivalent worldwide, not a competitor improvement, a category leap.”
“We sit beneath payment networks, not against them. We are the identity layer, not a payment product.”
Never say
Never call it a “polygraph” to investors; use “sincerity engine” or “honesty probability scoring”
Never say “like a lie detector but better”, it triggers legal and regulatory concerns immediately
Never say “we can tell if someone is lying”, say “we compute honesty probability from physiological markers”
Never promise a revenue number without citing which section of the briefing it comes from
Never say the sincerity score is “definitive”, always add “the 600-749 band routes to human review”
Never describe the technology as “AI-based face scanning”, it is sub-dermal haemodynamic analysis
Never position as competing with Visa or Mastercard; we are complementary infrastructure beneath them
