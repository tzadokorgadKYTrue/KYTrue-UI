The biological identity credential

What is a kyt_id?

A kyt_id is a cryptographically signed, short-lived credential that proves a specific living human was present, verified, and scored at a given moment. It is not a biometric template. It contains no facial data, no blood-flow pattern, no raw signal of any kind.

Think of it as a notarised biological timestamp, anchored to physiology instead of a private key. It can be presented to any relying party without revealing any underlying biometric.

Unlike a document-based KYC token that can be replayed or stolen, the kyt_id is bound to a session hash and expires by design. Re-presenting a credential from a prior session proves nothing, a a fresh one takes under 50 seconds.

Regulatory guidance on Layer 03, Sincerity Engine

KYTrue™'s Sincerity Engine (Layer 03) delivers a probabilistic honesty signal derived from haemodynamic stress, autonomic reflex, and behavioural incongruence. It is not a polygraph and does not make binary truth/lie determinations. It produces a continuous probability score.

Use of Layer 03 output is jurisdiction-dependent. KYTrue™ provides the signal; compliance with local law governing its use is the responsibility of the relying party.

Layers 01 and 02 (liveness and identity verification) are fully independent of Layer 03 and carry no polygraph-related regulatory concerns. Regulated financial institutions may use Layers 01+02 without any Layer 03 integration.
